## 1. Brand Essence

### 1.1 Positioning Statement

Mikael Caetano is a clinical psychologist who combines traditional therapeutic foundations with innovative techniques -- Clinical Hypnosis, NLP, and Time Line Therapy -- to facilitate real, lasting transformation. He is not a conventional therapist; he is a guide for people ready to break patterns, overcome deep-rooted fears, and rewrite their personal narratives.

**In one line:** _Where science meets intuition -- psychology that actually transforms._

### 1.2 Brand Personality

Five defining adjectives:

1. **Transformative** -- The core promise. Every touchpoint communicates the possibility of deep change.
2. **Grounded** -- Despite working with techniques many consider "alternative," Mikael is registered (CRP), evidence-informed, and ethically rigorous.
3. **Warm** -- Approachable, empathetic, human. Not cold clinical -- genuinely caring.
4. **Modern** -- Contemporary techniques, online availability, current visual language. Not stuck in 1990s therapy aesthetics.
5. **Holistic** -- Mind, body, and timeline. The approach treats the whole person, not just symptoms.

### 1.3 Brand Voice & Tone

**Voice** (consistent personality):
- First person when appropriate, creating intimacy
- Confident but never arrogant
- Uses accessible language -- avoids excessive clinical jargon
- Speaks with authority from experience, not from hierarchy
- Balances professionalism with genuine warmth

**Tone** (adapts by context):

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Hero / Headlines | Inspiring, bold, direct | _"Transforme o que te limita em força para avançar."_ |
| Service descriptions | Clear, informative, reassuring | _"A Hipnose Clínica permite acessar padrões inconscientes de forma segura e ética."_ |
| About / Bio | Personal, warm, credible | _"Acredito na importância de oferecer um trabalho ético e comprometido."_ |
| CTA / Contact | Encouraging, low-pressure | _"Dê o primeiro passo. Agende uma conversa."_ |
| Social proof | Genuine, emotional | _"Mudanças reais acontecem todos os dias neste consultório."_ |

### 1.4 Brand Values

- **Ética** -- Professional responsibility above all
- **Resultado** -- Commitment to measurable, lasting change
- **Respeito** -- Deep regard for each person's pace and story
- **Inovação** -- Willingness to use what truly works, even if unconventional
- **Acolhimento** -- Every interaction should feel safe

## 2. Color Palette

### 2.1 Design Philosophy

The palette draws from the intersection of **deep forest** and **warm earth** -- reflecting Mikael's connection to nature (visible in his Instagram content featuring waterfalls, outdoor rituals, and natural settings) while maintaining the sophistication expected of a healthcare professional. We deliberately avoid generic "therapist blue" and clinical white.

The system uses a **dark-anchored** palette with warm accents: deep, rich backgrounds that create intimacy and premium feel, balanced by warm golden tones that evoke transformation and light.

### 2.2 Primary Colors

#### Deep Forest -- `#1B3A2D`
- **RGB:** 27, 58, 45
- **HSL:** 155, 36%, 17%
- **Usage:** Primary brand color. Hero backgrounds, navigation, footer, section accents.
- **Psychology:** Trust, depth, growth, groundedness. Forest green connects to nature and renewal without feeling clinical.
- **Contrast on white:** 13.6:1 (AAA)

#### Warm Sage -- `#5B7B6A`
- **RGB:** 91, 123, 106
- **HSL:** 148, 15%, 42%
- **Usage:** Secondary brand color. Subheadings, borders, hover states, supporting elements.
- **Psychology:** Calm, balance, organic warmth. The desaturated tone adds sophistication.
- **Contrast on white:** 4.6:1 (AA)
- **Contrast on cream `#FAF7F2`:** 4.4:1 (AA for large text)

### 2.3 Accent Colors

#### Golden Hour -- `#C4943A`
- **RGB:** 196, 148, 58
- **HSL:** 39, 54%, 50%
- **Usage:** Primary accent. CTAs, highlights, "transformation" moments, hover accents, decorative elements.
- **Psychology:** Warmth, illumination, change, value. Gold connects to premium quality and the "light" of insight.
- **Contrast on Deep Forest: 6.2:1 (AA)**
- **Note:** Use sparingly -- this is the "spark" color. Maximum 10-15% of any given view.

#### Burnished Gold -- `#A67B2E`
- **RGB:** 166, 123, 46
- **HSL:** 38, 57%, 42%
- **Usage:** Darker gold variant for text on light backgrounds, active states, links.
- **Contrast on white:** 4.5:1 (AA)
- **Contrast on cream:** 4.3:1 (AA for large text)

#### Soft Terracotta -- `#B8785A`
- **RGB:** 184, 120, 90
- **HSL:** 19, 38%, 54%
- **Usage:** Tertiary warm accent. Testimonial cards, warm section backgrounds (at low opacity), decorative borders.
- **Psychology:** Earth, warmth, human touch. Adds variety without breaking the nature palette.
- **Contrast on Deep Forest: 4.8:1 (AA)**

### 2.4 Neutral Colors

#### Ivory Cream -- `#FAF7F2`
- **RGB:** 250, 247, 242
- **HSL:** 38, 47%, 96%
- **Usage:** Primary background. Warmer than pure white -- eliminates the clinical feel.
- **Note:** This is NOT white. The warm undertone is deliberate and essential to the brand feel.

#### Warm White -- `#F5F0E8`
- **RGB:** 245, 240, 232
- **HSL:** 37, 43%, 94%
- **Usage:** Alternating section background. Cards on cream background. Creates subtle depth.

#### Parchment -- `#EDE6DA`
- **RGB:** 237, 230, 218
- **HSL:** 38, 35%, 89%
- **Usage:** Borders, dividers, card backgrounds on warm-white sections. Input field backgrounds.

#### Stone -- `#B5AD9E`
- **RGB:** 181, 173, 158
- **HSL:** 39, 12%, 66%
- **Usage:** Muted text, placeholders, disabled states, subtle borders.
- **Contrast on cream:** 2.6:1 (decorative only, not for meaningful text)

#### Charcoal -- `#2C2C2C`
- **RGB:** 44, 44, 44
- **HSL:** 0, 0%, 17%
- **Usage:** Primary body text color. Rich black that's softer than pure #000.
- **Contrast on cream:** 14.5:1 (AAA)

#### Deep Charcoal -- `#1A1A1A`
- **RGB:** 26, 26, 26
- **HSL:** 0, 0%, 10%
- **Usage:** Headings on light backgrounds. Maximum contrast for display type.
- **Contrast on cream:** 16.8:1 (AAA)

### 2.5 Semantic Colors

#### Success -- `#3D7A5F`
- **RGB:** 61, 122, 95
- **HSL:** 153, 33%, 36%
- **Usage:** Success states, confirmation messages, positive indicators.
- **Contrast on cream:** 5.3:1 (AA)

#### Warning -- `#C4943A`
- **RGB:** 196, 148, 58 (reuses Golden Hour)
- **Usage:** Warning states, attention indicators.

#### Error -- `#B54B4B`
- **RGB:** 181, 75, 75
- **HSL:** 0, 42%, 50%
- **Usage:** Error states, validation errors, destructive actions.
- **Contrast on cream:** 4.8:1 (AA)

#### Info -- `#4A7B8C`
- **RGB:** 74, 123, 140
- **HSL:** 195, 31%, 42%
- **Usage:** Informational messages, help text, tooltips.
- **Contrast on cream:** 4.5:1 (AA)

### 2.6 Color Usage Guidelines

```
Background composition (typical page):
  60% -- Ivory Cream / Warm White (alternating sections)
  25% -- Deep Forest (hero, footer, accent sections)
  10% -- Parchment / Stone (cards, borders, subtle elements)
   5% -- Golden Hour / Terracotta (CTAs, highlights, accents)
```

**Rules:**
1. Never use pure white (`#FFFFFF`) or pure black (`#000000`) as backgrounds.
2. Golden Hour is reserved for interactive elements and key highlights. Do not use it for large background areas.
3. Deep Forest text on Ivory Cream is the primary dark-on-light combination.
4. Ivory Cream text on Deep Forest is the primary light-on-dark combination.
5. Always verify contrast ratios when combining non-standard pairs.
6. The Soft Terracotta is used at 8-12% opacity for warm background washes on cards.

### 2.7 Gradient Definitions

#### Hero Gradient (Dark)
```css
background: linear-gradient(135deg, #1B3A2D 0%, #142E23 50%, #1B3A2D 100%);
```
Subtle variation within the Deep Forest for depth. Avoid flat single-color hero blocks.

#### Warm Glow (Decorative)
```css
background: radial-gradient(ellipse at 30% 50%, rgba(196, 148, 58, 0.08) 0%, transparent 70%);
```
Used as an overlay on dark sections to create a gentle warm atmosphere. Never as a standalone background.

#### Section Transition
```css
background: linear-gradient(180deg, #FAF7F2 0%, #F5F0E8 100%);
```
Smooth transition between alternating light sections. Eliminates harsh background-color boundaries.

## 3. Typography

### 3.1 Design Philosophy

The type system pairs a refined serif for headlines with a humanist sans-serif for body text. This creates the tension between **elegance and approachability** that defines the brand: sophisticated enough to convey expertise, warm enough to feel welcoming.

### 3.2 Font Families

#### Display / Headings: **DM Serif Display**
- **Source:** [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/DM+Serif+Display)
- **Weights used:** 400 (Regular)
- **Character:** Elegant, editorial, premium. High contrast between thick and thin strokes gives it a refined, magazine-quality feel.
- **Why this font:** DM Serif Display brings gravitas without stuffiness. It reads as "thoughtful expert" rather than "academic institution." The slightly warm lettershapes (notice the rounded terminals) prevent it from feeling cold.
- **Fallback:** `Georgia, "Times New Roman", serif`

#### Body / UI: **Inter**
- **Source:** [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter)
- **Weights used:** 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (Semi-Bold)
- **Character:** Clean, highly legible, modern. Designed specifically for screens with excellent readability at all sizes.
- **Why this font:** Inter is the gold standard for screen readability. Its large x-height and open letterforms ensure body text is effortless to read. The wide weight range allows for clear hierarchy without changing fonts.
- **Fallback:** `"Helvetica Neue", Arial, sans-serif`

#### Monospace (for CRP, phone numbers, credentials): **JetBrains Mono**
- **Source:** [Google Fonts](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/JetBrains+Mono)
- **Weights used:** 400 (Regular)
- **Character:** Technical, credible, precise.
- **Usage:** CRP registration number, phone number formatting, any data that benefits from tabular alignment.
- **Fallback:** `"SF Mono", Consolas, monospace`

### 3.3 Google Fonts Loading

```html
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.googleapis.com">
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.gstatic.com" crossorigin>
<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=DM+Serif+Display&family=Inter:wght@300;400;500;600&family=JetBrains+Mono&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">
```

**Performance note:** Use `font-display: swap` (included in the URL via `display=swap`) to prevent FOIT (Flash of Invisible Text). The fallback fonts are chosen to have similar metrics to minimize layout shift.

### 3.4 Type Scale

Base size: `16px` (1rem). Scale ratio: ~1.25 (Major Third) for body, ~1.333 (Perfect Fourth) for display sizes.

| Token | Size | rem | Weight | Line Height | Letter Spacing | Usage |
|-------|------|-----|--------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| `--font-size-display` | 56px | 3.5rem | 400 | 1.1 | -0.02em | Hero headline (desktop) |
| `--font-size-h1` | 44px | 2.75rem | 400 | 1.15 | -0.015em | Page titles, section heroes |
| `--font-size-h2` | 36px | 2.25rem | 400 | 1.2 | -0.01em | Section headings |
| `--font-size-h3` | 28px | 1.75rem | 400 | 1.25 | -0.005em | Subsection headings |
| `--font-size-h4` | 22px | 1.375rem | 500 | 1.3 | 0 | Card titles, feature headings |
| `--font-size-h5` | 18px | 1.125rem | 600 | 1.4 | 0.01em | Small headings, labels |
| `--font-size-body-lg` | 18px | 1.125rem | 400 | 1.7 | 0 | Lead paragraphs, intro text |
| `--font-size-body` | 16px | 1rem | 400 | 1.7 | 0 | Default body text |
| `--font-size-body-sm` | 14px | 0.875rem | 400 | 1.6 | 0.005em | Secondary text, captions |
| `--font-size-caption` | 12px | 0.75rem | 500 | 1.5 | 0.02em | Legal text, metadata, CRP |
| `--font-size-overline` | 11px | 0.6875rem | 600 | 1.4 | 0.1em | Section labels, categories (uppercase) |

### 3.5 Responsive Typography

All display and heading sizes scale down on smaller viewports using `clamp()`:

```css
--font-size-display: clamp(2.25rem, 5vw + 1rem, 3.5rem);   /* 36px -> 56px */
--font-size-h1: clamp(2rem, 4vw + 0.75rem, 2.75rem);        /* 32px -> 44px */
--font-size-h2: clamp(1.75rem, 3vw + 0.5rem, 2.25rem);      /* 28px -> 36px */
--font-size-h3: clamp(1.5rem, 2vw + 0.5rem, 1.75rem);       /* 24px -> 28px */
```

Body text remains fixed at `1rem` (16px) on all viewports. Never go below 14px for any readable text.

### 3.6 Typography Rules

1. **Headings** (h1-h3) always use **DM Serif Display** at weight 400. Never bold a serif display font -- it breaks the elegant stroke contrast.
2. **Headings** (h4-h5) use **Inter** at Medium (500) or Semi-Bold (600) for hierarchy within content sections.
3. **Body text** uses **Inter** at Regular (400). Use Medium (500) for emphasis within paragraphs sparingly.
4. **Overline labels** (section categories like "HIPNOSE CLINICA", "SOBRE") use **Inter** at Semi-Bold (600), uppercase, with wide letter-spacing (`0.1em`).
5. **Maximum line length:** 65-75 characters for body text. Use `max-width: 65ch` on paragraph containers.
6. **Paragraph spacing:** `1.5em` margin-bottom between paragraphs. `0.5em` between heading and following paragraph.
7. **No underlines** on links by default. Use color change + subtle border-bottom or weight change for link indication.

## 5. Shadows, Borders & Effects

### 5.1 Shadow Scale

Shadows use warm-tinted values (not pure black) to maintain the brand's warm aesthetic:

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| `--shadow-xs` | `0 1px 2px rgba(27, 58, 45, 0.05)` | Subtle depth on inputs, small elements |
| `--shadow-sm` | `0 2px 8px rgba(27, 58, 45, 0.08)` | Cards at rest, dropdowns |
| `--shadow-md` | `0 4px 16px rgba(27, 58, 45, 0.10)` | Cards on hover, elevated elements |
| `--shadow-lg` | `0 8px 32px rgba(27, 58, 45, 0.12)` | Modals, floating elements |
| `--shadow-xl` | `0 16px 48px rgba(27, 58, 45, 0.16)` | Hero image overlays, feature highlights |
| `--shadow-inner` | `inset 0 2px 4px rgba(27, 58, 45, 0.06)` | Pressed buttons, input focus inner glow |

**Note:** Shadow color uses Deep Forest RGB (`27, 58, 45`) instead of black. This creates shadows with a subtle green undertone that feel cohesive with the palette.

### 5.2 Border Radius Scale

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| `--radius-sm` | 6px | Inputs, small buttons, tags |
| `--radius-md` | 10px | Cards, panels, medium elements |
| `--radius-lg` | 16px | Large cards, modal dialogs |
| `--radius-xl` | 24px | Feature images, hero elements |
| `--radius-full` | 9999px | Circular avatars, pill buttons, badges |

**Note:** We avoid overly rounded corners (no border-radius: 50px on rectangles). The radius values are moderate, conveying "refined" rather than "playful."

### 5.3 Border Styles

```css
--border-thin: 1px solid var(--color-border);
--border-medium: 2px solid var(--color-border);
--border-accent: 2px solid var(--color-accent);
--border-decorative: 1px solid rgba(196, 148, 58, 0.3);  /* Subtle gold line */
```

### 5.4 Transitions

```css
--transition-fast: 150ms ease;        /* Hover color changes, opacity */
--transition-base: 250ms ease;        /* Standard interactions */
--transition-slow: 400ms ease;        /* Layout shifts, reveals */
--transition-spring: 500ms cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1);  /* Playful bounces (use sparingly) */
```

**Animation philosophy:** Subtle, purposeful motion. Animations should feel like gentle breaths, not fireworks. Primary motion patterns:
- Fade in + slight upward translate on scroll-reveal (`translateY(20px)` to `translateY(0)`)
- Smooth color transitions on hover
- Gentle scale on card hover (`scale(1.02)`)
- No spinning, bouncing, or aggressive attention-grabbing animations

## 8. Visual Direction

### 8.1 Photography Style

**Primary photos (of Mikael):**
- Warm, natural lighting -- golden hour or soft indoor lighting
- Genuine expressions, not staged "corporate headshot" poses
- Environments: bookshelves (his Instagram already has this), nature, warm office setting
- Shallow depth of field for professional quality
- Color grading: slightly warm, desaturated greens and browns, never oversaturated

**Secondary photos (atmosphere):**
- Nature imagery: forests, water, organic textures (aligns with his Reiki/holistic interests)
- Abstract calm: soft-focus foliage, light filtering through leaves, water ripples
- Never: generic stock photos of hands-holding, brain diagrams, or couches

**Photo treatment:**
- Apply a subtle warm overlay (`rgba(196, 148, 58, 0.05)`) to unify photos with the palette
- Use `mix-blend-mode: multiply` on a Deep Forest overlay for dark photo sections
- Photos should feel lived-in and authentic, never overly retouched

### 8.2 Icon Style

**Style:** Line icons, 1.5px stroke weight, rounded caps and joins.

**Recommended icon set:** [Lucide Icons](https://lucide.dev/) -- open source, consistent, beautifully minimal.

**Icon usage:**
- Service icons: `Brain` (Hipnose), `Lightbulb` (PNL), `Clock` (TLT -- Timeline)
- Contact: `Phone`, `MapPin`, `Calendar`
- Social: `Instagram`
- UI: `Menu`, `X`, `ChevronDown`, `ArrowRight`
- Size: 24px for inline, 32px for cards, 48px for feature highlights
- Color: Golden Hour on light backgrounds, Ivory Cream on dark backgrounds

**Alternative custom icons for services:**
Consider commissioning or creating simple, elegant custom icons for the three specialties:
- **Hipnose:** A stylized spiral or concentric circles (representing the hypnotic focus)
- **PNL:** An eye with neural pathways (perception and rewiring)
- **TLT:** A flowing timeline with a transformation point (past to future)

### 8.3 Textures & Decorative Elements

**Organic noise texture:**
A very subtle grain overlay on hero sections and dark backgrounds adds analog warmth and prevents the "flat digital" feel. Implementation:

```css
.texture-grain::after {
  content: "";
  position: absolute;
  inset: 0;
  background-image: url("data:image/svg+xml,..."); /* tiny noise SVG */
  opacity: 0.03;
  pointer-events: none;
  mix-blend-mode: overlay;
}
```

**Decorative line:**
A thin golden line (1px, `--color-accent` at 30% opacity) used as a section separator or accent element. Horizontal, 80px wide, centered.

```css
.decorative-line {
  width: 80px;
  height: 1px;
  background: linear-gradient(90deg, transparent, var(--color-accent), transparent);
  margin: var(--space-8) auto;
}
```

**Quote marks:**
For testimonials, use an oversized decorative quotation mark in DM Serif Display, colored Golden Hour at 15% opacity, positioned as a background element.

### 8.4 Scroll Animations

Use `IntersectionObserver` for scroll-triggered animations. Keep them minimal:

```css
.reveal {
  opacity: 0;
  transform: translateY(24px);
  transition: opacity 0.6s ease, transform 0.6s ease;
}

.reveal--visible {
  opacity: 1;
  transform: translateY(0);
}
```

**Stagger children** by 100ms each for lists and grids:
```css
.reveal-stagger > :nth-child(1) { transition-delay: 0ms; }
.reveal-stagger > :nth-child(2) { transition-delay: 100ms; }
.reveal-stagger > :nth-child(3) { transition-delay: 200ms; }
```
